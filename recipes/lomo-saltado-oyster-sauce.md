## Ingredients

### Sauce
- 4 tbsp soy sauce (low sodium)
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 1 tbsp aji amarillo paste

### Stir-fry
- 🥩 500g sirloin or filet, sliced thin against the grain
- 🧅 1 red onion, sliced into thick wedges
- 🍅 2 tomatoes, cut into wedges
- 🧄 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 🫗 1–2 tbsp neutral oil (vegetable, canola, avocado)
- 🥔 2 large potatoes, cut into fries
- 🌿 Fresh cilantro, chopped

## Steps

1. **Marinate beef:** toss sliced beef with a splash of soy sauce and half the garlic. Let sit 5–7 min while you prep the rest
2. **Mix sauce:** combine soy sauce, oyster sauce, vinegar, and aji amarillo paste in a small bowl
3. **Cook potatoes:** fry or air-fry until crispy, set aside
4. **Sear beef:** heat oil until shimmering. Sear beef in batches, **1–2 min per side only**. Pull while still pink — it finishes later. Remove and set aside
5. **Stir-fry veg:** add onion, cook 1–2 min. Add remaining garlic and tomatoes, cook 1 min
6. **Combine:** return beef to the pan, pour in sauce, toss 30 seconds
7. **Serve:** garnish with cilantro, serve with rice and crispy potatoes on the side

## Notes

- Skip cornstarch in the sauce for a thinner, restaurant-style finish
- **Aji amarillo substitutes:**
  - **Best:** Cholula chili-garlic sauce — 1–2 tsp
  - **Alternative:** pickled jalapeños — 1–2 tbsp chopped (reduce vinegar slightly)
  - **Not ideal:** chili crisp — too oily for this sauce
  - **Hack:** 1 tsp Cholula + ½ tsp chili crisp for extra aromatic crunch
- The wok must be very hot for proper searing
